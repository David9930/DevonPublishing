Living with the Ghost of Sam: A Supernatural Comedy Series

A unique blend of comedy and the supernatural, exploring the bizarre connection between Derek and his deceased comedian cousin.

Spoiler Warning: This blog contains plot details from Episodes 1 & 2

The Premise

Based on the provided excerpts from "Living with the Ghost of Sam" Episodes 1 and 2, the story centers around Derek Kinison, the cousin of the late comedian Sam Kinison, and his wife Nancy Hammond, as they unexpectedly find themselves embroiled in a supernatural competition to win ownership of a mysterious Victorian mansion with strong ties to Sam's final days.

Derek experiences a dramatic corporate meltdown that mirrors his late cousin Sam Kinison's explosive comedic style, suggesting a spiritual awakening or influence. This event acts as a catalyst for Derek to question his superficial corporate life and reconnect with a long-dormant desire for change.

As the story unfolds, supernatural occurrences begin to manifest, seemingly linked to Sam Kinison's ghost, including fleeting appearances in mirrors, self-activating objects (pipe organ, toaster, coffee maker), and unexplained weather phenomena like thunder in clear skies.

"Changes have consequences, and consequences make change!"

The competition is set when Derek and Nancy encounter social media influencers Chad Wellington and Marina St. Clair, who are also interested in buying the mansion. This setup creates a perfect stage for both comedy and supernatural encounters.

Episode 1: "Leap of Faith"

TIMELINE: PAST

The journey begins with Derek's corporate meltdown and the discovery of Ravencrest Mansion, a weathered Victorian mansion with a history as a psychiatric hospital and later a comedy club called "The Laugh Asylum".

Key Revelations:

Nancy initially finds a painting of the mansion, sparking Derek's memory of a past dream. They meet Linda Rhodes, a mysterious realtor with hidden knowledge about the mansion's history. A red pentagram painted on the foyer floor hints at darker elements. The discovery of a comedy book purportedly by Sam Kinison published the month he died. The previous owner of the mansion died on the same day as Sam.

A high-stakes overnight challenge is proposed by the current owner through Linda, with the winner being the one who lasts until dawn. This dramatic proposition raises the stakes and promises supernatural confrontations.

The Competition Unfolds

Episode 2, "Night Moves," opens with a mysterious "Temporal Observation System" monitoring Derek's past, revealing early signs of a connection to Sam and a later meeting with Nancy. The system notes a "genetic component" and "timeline convergence probability," suggesting a predetermined link between the Kinison and Blackwood bloodlines.

Episode 2: "Night Moves"

TIMELINE: PRESENT

Derek and Nancy prepare for the overnight stay, gathering supplies and encountering Earl, a local shopkeeper with cryptic knowledge about the mansion's history, including its past as a comedy venue where Sam Kinison performed his last show on April 9th, 1992.

Supernatural Escalation:

The mansion becomes more active as the night progresses. Sam's voice can be heard through various devices, and the mansion attempts to communicate through shifting images on a comedy poster. Meanwhile, Chad and Marina face personalized supernatural torments that expose the superficiality of their relationship through the mansion's mirrors.

While the influencers' relationship implodes under the supernatural pressure, Derek and Nancy's bond strengthens as they navigate the strange events together, showing genuine connection and support for each other.

The episode ends with Chad and Marina's hasty departure from the mansion, leaving Derek and Nancy as the apparent winners of the overnight challenge. They decide to stay and uncover the secrets of Ravencrest, with the final scene showing Sam's ghost seemingly approving of their presence.

Ever Wished for a Do-Over?

Dive into "A Letter Guide For Rebecca"

Published: October 15, 2023

What if you could live your life again? Not just relive it, but actively change it, armed with the knowledge of everything that went wrong the first time around? Derek Devon's "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" dives headfirst into this tantalizing premise, blending science fiction, romance, and a healthy dose of regret into a unique narrative presented as a deeply personal guidebook.

What's It About?

The story unfolds through the eyes (and memories) of Derek Devon – or rather, Derek 2.0. He's somehow been given the extraordinary chance to return to his youth, his mind filled with the experiences and knowledge of his first life, which stretched over a century.

This isn't just about correcting youthful mistakes, like awkward school dances or missed opportunities, although those are certainly part of it. Derek 2.0 is on a mission, driven by a profound love for Rebecca (his "It-girl" across two lifetimes) and haunted by the regrets of his past.

He sees this second chance as an opportunity to right wrongs, both personal and potentially world-altering. He uses his foreknowledge to navigate events, aiming to reshape outcomes. But as the narrative cautions, "Changes have consequences Rebecca and consequences make change!"

Derek's memories span over a century! Imagine the perspective...

A Glimpse Inside: Altering Fate at Gibson's Pond

Original Timeline

A seemingly innocent afternoon of playing hooky and swinging on a rope swing ended in tragedy for Rebecca's brother, Todd.

Altered Timeline

Derek 2.0 manages to grab the rope just as it snaps, altering the trajectory of Todd's fall and changing a deadly outcome into a painful but survivable landing.

Without giving away too much, one poignant example of Derek's mission involves an incident at Gibson's Pond. In his original timeline, a seemingly innocent afternoon of playing hooky and swinging on a rope swing ended in tragedy for Rebecca's brother, Todd.

Armed with this devastating foreknowledge, Derek 2.0 finds himself back at the pond on that fateful day. He knows the exact moment the rope will break and the potentially fatal consequences. The tension builds as he positions himself not just to witness the event, but to intervene.

In a heart-stopping sequence, he manages to grab the rope just as it snaps, altering the trajectory of Todd's fall and changing a deadly outcome into a painful but survivable landing. This single act highlights the core of Derek's journey: using his unique knowledge to protect those he cares about and wrestling with the immense responsibility that comes with his power.

Why Should You Read It?

"A Letter Guide For Rebecca" offers a compelling blend for readers who enjoy:

Unique Narrative Structures

The book's format as a guide written by the protagonist to the love of his life is unconventional and intriguing.

Time Travel with a Twist

It explores the classic "what if" scenario of reliving one's life, focusing on personal redemption and the butterfly effect of changing the past.

Sci-Fi Elements

While grounded in relatable emotions like love and regret, the story incorporates science fiction themes, including mysterious encounters and advanced technology.

Exploring Deep Themes

It delves into the nature of regret, the weight of choices, the complexities of love across time, and the ethics of wielding power over fate.

If you've ever pondered second chances or wondered how different life could be if you knew then what you know now, "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" provides a thought-provoking and emotionally resonant journey. It's a story about fixing the past, facing the consequences, and fighting for love across more than one lifetime.
