Ever Wished for a Do-Over? Dive into "A Letter Guide For Rebecca"

What if you could live your life again, armed with the knowledge of everything that went wrong the first time?

By Devon Publishing
April 26, 2025
Novel Science Fiction Second Chances

What if you could live your life again? Not just relive it, but actively change it, armed with the knowledge of everything that went wrong the first time around? Derek Devon's "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" dives headfirst into this tantalizing premise, blending science fiction, romance, and a healthy dose of regret into a unique narrative presented as a deeply personal guidebook.

What's It About?

The story unfolds through the eyes (and memories) of Derek Devon – or rather, Derek 2.0. He's somehow been given the extraordinary chance to return to his youth, his mind filled with the experiences and knowledge of his first life. This isn't just about correcting youthful mistakes like awkward school dances or missed opportunities, although those are certainly part of it. Derek is on a mission, driven by a profound love for Rebecca (his "It-girl" across two lifetimes) and haunted by the regrets of his past.

"You are about to discover how far-reaching those choices I made have changed the world as 'we' could have known it and how I used to know it. You're the sole keeper of this knowledge now."
— Derek Devon, A Letter Guide For Rebecca

As Derek prepares his sailboat 'It-girl' for a circumnavigation in his later years, he reveals the extraordinary truth behind his seemingly uncanny abilities to predict future events. These weren't just lucky guesses but knowledge gained from having lived through time before. Through the format of a two-volume guide addressed to Rebecca, Derek shares his journey of using this foreknowledge to reshape outcomes both personal and potentially world-altering.

A Glimpse Inside: Altering Fate at Gibson's Pond

Without giving away too much, one poignant example of Derek's mission involves an incident at Gibson's Pond. In his original timeline, a seemingly innocent afternoon of playing hooky and swinging on a rope swing ended in tragedy for Rebecca's brother, Todd.

Armed with this devastating foreknowledge, Derek 2.0 finds himself back at the pond on that fateful day. He knows the exact moment the rope will break and the potentially fatal consequences. The tension builds as he positions himself not just to witness the event, but to intervene.

In a heart-stopping sequence, he manages to grab the rope just as it snaps, altering the trajectory of Todd's fall and changing a deadly outcome into a painful but survivable landing. This single act highlights the core of Derek's journey: using his unique knowledge to protect those he cares about and wrestling with the immense responsibility that comes with his power.

"I just made first contact, and I wasn't going to let them see me wail like I did that day in the Gremlin when you broke up with me. No way, I am far too proud to expose that to my new alien friends, but what happened next was truly, a gift of a lifetime."
— From "A Letter Guide For Rebecca"

With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility

Derek constantly grapples with the ethical implications of his foreknowledge. Should he prevent major historical tragedies? Make himself wealthy through lottery numbers or sporting bets? Or focus on personal relationships?

As he navigates his second chance at adolescence, he must weigh the consequences of every intervention. The novel raises profound questions about the butterfly effect of our choices and the moral burden of possessing such extraordinary power.

Derek cites the familiar refrain, "With great power, comes great responsibility," acknowledging both the weight and privilege of his unique situation. His journey becomes as much about ethical navigation as it does about altering specific events.

Beyond Our World

While grounded in real locations and experiences, the novel incorporates elements that transcend conventional understanding. Derek's encounters with entities from beyond our world add a cosmic dimension to his personal journey.

The author hints that if you "strip away the sci-fi stuff," much of the story is based on real places and experiences. This blending of the familiar with the fantastical creates a narrative that feels both grounded and transcendent.

As one section of the book teases: "If you think we are alone in the Universe, THINK AGAIN! And what happens when a simple emotional desire to try and win the heart of your high school sweetheart infects our alien visitors? And not in a good way..."

The Heart of the Story

At its emotional core, "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" is a love story—one that spans multiple versions of reality. Derek's deep and enduring affection for Rebecca (his "It-girl" across lifetimes) drives many of his actions throughout his "do-over" life.

The author himself acknowledges: "Finally, the story is in fact a love story. My love story, and it is not yet finished... While my journey continues to find a new 'It-Girl', my current 'It-Girl' remains firmly etched in my memories.... Now, never to be forgotten!"

From their first skinny dipping experience at Hermitage Pond to small moments of connection throughout their lives, the novel celebrates the persistence of love across time and circumstance, asking whether some connections are powerful enough to transcend even the most radical alterations to reality.

What Would You Do With a Do-Over?

The novel poses fascinating "what if" questions that readers can't help but apply to their own lives:

Prevent Tragedies

Would you try to stop 9/11 from happening? Save Princess Diana? How would you approach the immense responsibility of preventing large-scale tragedies?

Personal Gain

Would you bet on sporting events with guaranteed outcomes? Memorize lottery numbers? Use your knowledge to accumulate wealth and power?

Lost Love

Would you try again with "the one that got away"? How would you approach romance knowing what you know now, but in your younger body?

Second Chances

Which personal regrets would you prioritize fixing? Would you take different educational or career paths? How would you balance your old desires with your mature perspective?

The Butterfly Effect

How would you handle the knowledge that even small changes could have massive unforeseen consequences? As the novel warns, "Changes have consequences, Rebecca, and consequences make change!"

The Burden of Knowledge

How would you cope with knowing future tragedies and deaths of loved ones? Could you maintain your sanity while carrying the weight of future knowledge?

The Letter Guide

In his later years, Derek creates his "Letter Guide For Rebecca," finally revealing the truth behind his uncanny knowledge and the choices he made across two lifetimes.

Why Should You Read It?

"A Letter Guide For Rebecca" offers a compelling blend for readers who enjoy:

Unique Narrative Structures

The book's format as a guide written by the protagonist to the love of his life is unconventional and intriguing. Through this format, readers gain intimate access to Derek's thoughts, regrets, and hopes across multiple timelines.

Time Travel with a Twist

It explores the classic "what if" scenario of reliving one's life, focusing on personal redemption and the butterfly effect of changing the past. Unlike typical time travel stories that focus on major historical events, this narrative centers on the personal—how one man's choices ripple outward to affect those he loves.

Sci-Fi Elements

While grounded in relatable emotions like love and regret, the story incorporates science fiction themes, including mysterious encounters and advanced technology. The author even hints that much of the story is based on real places and experiences, "if you strip away the sci-fi stuff."

Exploring Deep Themes

It delves into the nature of regret, the weight of choices, the complexities of love across time, and the ethics of wielding power over fate. The novel asks profound questions about responsibility, free will, and whether some connections are powerful enough to transcend even the most significant disruptions to reality.

"Finally, the story is in fact a love story. My love story, and it is not yet finished... While my journey continues to find a new 'It-Girl', my current 'It-Girl' remains firmly etched in my memories.... Now, never to be forgotten!"
— From the author's note

Have You Ever Wished for a Second Chance?

If you've ever pondered second chances or wondered how different life could be if you knew then what you know now, "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" provides a thought-provoking and emotionally resonant journey. It's a story about fixing the past, facing the consequences, and fighting for love across more than one lifetime.
