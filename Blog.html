A Letter Guide For Rebecca

A journey through time, memory, and the choices that shape our reality

By Derek Devon
April 26, 2025
Fiction Science Fiction Time Travel

In the quiet moments before setting sail on my Morgan Out Island for a circumnavigation, I find myself reflecting on the journey that led to writing "A Letter Guide For Rebecca." What began as personal reflections—a desire to capture precious moments from my past—evolved into something far more complex and extraordinary.

This novel explores the boundaries between reality and possibility, weaving together the threads of love, regret, and the profound desire for second chances. It poses questions about the nature of time, memory, and the ripple effects of our choices across the fabric of existence.

"You are about to discover how far-reaching those choices I made have changed the world as 'we' could have known it and how I used to know it. You're the sole keeper of this knowledge now."

The World of "A Letter Guide For Rebecca"

At its core, the novel is a personal confession from Derek Devon to Rebecca, his "it-girl" and the "love of his lives." Through the format of a two-volume guide, Derek reveals the extraordinary truth behind his seemingly uncanny abilities to predict future events—not luck, but knowledge gained from having lived through time before.

The narrative shifts between Derek's present (as he prepares for his sailing circumnavigation in his 60s) and his past as a young man, particularly around age 12-13 and later in high school. Readers are invited to unravel the mystery of how an adult consciousness found itself re-implanted in a younger body, and the ethical dilemmas that arise from possessing knowledge of future events.

A Second Chance at Life

Derek Devon has been given a remarkable opportunity—a "do-over" that allows him to relive his life from the age of 12, armed with the memories and wisdom of his adult self. This premise creates a fascinating exploration of how foreknowledge might be both a blessing and a burden.

As Derek navigates his second chance at adolescence, he must constantly weigh the consequences of his actions. Should he use his knowledge to prevent tragedies? To accumulate wealth? To win the heart of Rebecca earlier? Or would such interventions have unintended ripple effects across the timeline?

The novel explores these questions through Derek's candid reflections in his letter guide, creating a deeply personal account of an extraordinary circumstance.

Beyond Conventional Reality

While grounded in real locations and experiences, the novel incorporates elements that transcend conventional understanding. Derek's encounters with beings from the future, discussions about nanotechnology capable of manipulating cells at a microscopic level, and references to a "Prime Directive" all suggest a narrative that ventures beyond everyday reality.

These science fiction elements serve not merely as plot devices but as vehicles to explore profound questions about free will, determinism, and the nature of consciousness itself. Derek's interactions with voices he names Morpheus, Trinity, and Cypher (a nod to "The Matrix") provide a framework for examining these philosophical questions through a contemporary lens.

The novel's concept of "brain dumps" and timeline alterations challenges readers to reconsider their understanding of time as linear and fixed.

The Heart of the Story

At its emotional core, "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" is a love story—one that spans multiple versions of reality. Derek's deep and enduring affection for Rebecca drives many of his actions and choices throughout his "do-over" life.

From their first skinny dipping experience at Hermitage Pond to the small moments of connection shared throughout their lives, the novel celebrates the persistence of love across time and circumstance. Derek remembers the exact moment he thought, "You're going to marry her one day, Mrs. Rebecca Devon," revealing how central she is to his motivation.

The novel asks whether love is merely a product of chemical reactions in the brain, as Tank suggests when discussing the origin of Derek's "butterflies" feeling for Rebecca, or something more transcendent that can survive even the most radical alterations to reality.

The Key Characters

The world of "A Letter Guide For Rebecca" is populated by a rich cast of characters, each playing a role in Derek's journey through his altered timeline. Here are some of the most significant:

Derek Devon

The author of the "Letter Guide." With knowledge of future events and the ability to alter timelines, Derek navigates the ethical complexities of his "do-over" life while preparing for a sailboat circumnavigation in his present time.

Rebecca

The primary recipient of the "Letter Guide," referred to as "Dimples," "it-girl," and the "love of my lives." The guide reveals the truth about Derek's life and involves tasks for her to complete.

Tank

One of the voices Derek hears in his head, representing a future being or AI who can influence thoughts and feelings and provides explanations about the technology involved in the timeline alterations.

Tina

A girl Derek meets at a pre-high school party. Their relationship becomes a complex moral dilemma as Derek grapples with having adult knowledge in a young body.

Mr. Sunderland

A mysterious figure who seems to possess detailed knowledge of Derek's activities, possibly connected to intelligence operations or surveillance.

Morpheus, Trinity, Cypher

Names Derek assigns to different voices he hears during discussions about nanotechnology and other advanced concepts. The reference to "The Matrix" highlights Derek's love of sci-fi.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of the "Letter Guide for Rebecca"?

The guide serves as a form of confession, explanation, and a passing of knowledge from Derek Devon to Rebecca. It reveals that his seemingly uncanny ability to know what would happen was not luck but the result of living through time before. Derek aims to explain his past actions and the far-reaching consequences of the choices he made, entrusting Rebecca with this unique and potentially world-altering information.

What is the significance of the "Proofing Test"?

The "Proofing Test" is a pivotal moment where Derek challenges Rebecca to perform a seemingly inexplicable action—sticking her hand in water based on a specific number—to prove whether he is telling the truth or is delusional. This test, linked to their shared personal history ("Our Number"), is designed to provide undeniable evidence to Rebecca of the extraordinary nature of his claims.

Why is Derek preparing for a circumnavigation?

Derek is planning to retire in a couple of years, around the age of 65, and use his full pension to embark on a solo circumnavigation of the globe on his extensively renovated Morgan Out Island sailboat, "It-girl." The boat is equipped with substantial solar power and battery systems to support his needs while at sea, suggesting both a practical plan and perhaps a symbolic journey.

What are the science fiction elements in the novel?

The novel incorporates several science fiction elements, including time travel or altered realities, communication with future beings, nanotechnology capable of manipulating cells at a microscopic level, and references to concepts like the "Prime Directive" from Star Trek. These elements serve as vehicles to explore deeper themes about free will, determinism, and the nature of reality itself.

How does the novel explore ethical dilemmas?

The novel grapples with complex ethical questions about the responsible use of foreknowledge. Derek must decide whether to intervene in events to prevent tragedies, how to navigate relationships with people who don't know his true nature, and the moral implications of having an adult consciousness in a child's body. The phrase "With great power, comes great responsibility" appears in the text, highlighting Derek's awareness of the ethical weight of his unique situation.
